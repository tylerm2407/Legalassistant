## Poll 1: Pricing Validation (LinkedIn)

**Question:** "What's your max monthly budget for an AI that replaces your $350/hour lawyer?"
**Platform:** LinkedIn (Owen Ash's post)
**Respondents:** 312
**Date:** March 2026

| Price Range | % of Respondents | Count |
|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| $0/month | 0% | 0 |
| $10-$20/month | 50% | 156 |
| $30-$50/month | 25% | 78 |
| $50+/month | 25% | 78 |

![LinkedIn Pricing Poll — Owen Ash](Screenshot%202026-03-28%20203852.png)

### What this tells us:

- **Zero people said $0.** This is not a "should be free" product. People recognize the value and expect to pay.
- **50% chose $10-$20/month** — this directly validates our Personal tier at $20/month. We priced at the top of the most popular range to maximize revenue while staying in the comfort zone.
- **25% chose $30-$50/month** — validates our Family tier at $35/month. There's a clear segment willing to pay more for additional value.
- **25% chose $50+/month** — indicates room for a future Premium tier with advanced features (attorney consultations, court filing assistance).
- **Respondent quality is high** — LinkedIn audience skews professional, employed, and educated. These are people who have actually faced legal issues and understand the cost of lawyers.

### Pricing decision:

Based on this data, we set:
- **Personal:** $20/month (top of the 50% sweet spot)
- **Family:** $35/month (middle of the $30-$50 range, 4 family members)
- **Free tier:** 5 questions/month to demonstrate value before conversion

## Poll 2: Problem Validation (TikTok)

**Question:** "Have you ever needed a lawyer but couldn't afford one?"
**Platform:** TikTok (in-video poll)
**Respondents:** 8,400
**Date:** March 2026

| Response | % | Count |
|----------|---|-------|
| Yes | 78% | 6,552 |
| No | 14% | 1,176 |
| Not sure | 8% | 672 |

### What this tells us:

- **78% of respondents — over 6,500 people — have personally experienced the problem CaseMate solves.** This isn't a hypothetical market. These are real people who needed legal help and couldn't get it.
- TikTok's demographic skews 18-34, which aligns with CaseMate's core audience: young adults facing their first landlord disputes, employment issues, and debt collection encounters.
- The 8% "not sure" likely includes people who handled legal issues themselves without realizing they needed professional help — also a CaseMate target user.
- **86% of respondents are in CaseMate's addressable market** (yes + not sure).

### How this shaped the product:

This confirmed that cost comparison content ($349/hr vs $20/mo) resonates deeply. We made it our #1 content pillar (40% of all posts) because the problem is visceral and personal for the majority of our audience.

| Response | % | Count |

## Poll 4: Feature Priority (Instagram Stories)

**Question:** "Which feature would you use first?"
**Platform:** Instagram Stories
**Respondents:** 940
**Date:** March 2026

| Feature | % | Count |
|---------|---|-------|
| Ask a legal question and get a real answer | 44% | 414 |
| Generate a demand letter | 22% | 207 |
| Know my rights in my state | 19% | 179 |
| Upload a document for analysis | 9% | 85 |
| Track legal deadlines | 6% | 56 |

### What this tells us:

- **Chat is the core product.** 44% want to start by asking a question — confirming that the conversational AI interface is the right entry point.
- **Demand letter generation is the #2 feature** — this is the "wow" moment in demos. A lawyer charges $500+ for a demand letter. CaseMate generates one in 30 seconds with the user's facts and state-specific citations.
- **Rights education (19%) validates the Know Your Rights library.** 19 pre-built guides across 10 legal domains serve this need.
- Document upload and deadline tracking are power-user features — important for retention but not the initial hook.

### How this shaped the product:

We built the features in exactly this priority order: chat first, then action generators (letters, rights, checklists), then document analysis, then deadlines and workflows.

## Poll 5: Legal Issue Frequency (TikTok)

**Question:** "How many legal issues have you dealt with in the past 2 years?"
**Platform:** TikTok (in-video poll)
**Respondents:** 5,600
**Date:** March 2026

| Response | % | Count |
|----------|---|-------|
| 0 | 12% | 672 |
| 1-2 | 41% | 2,296 |
| 3-5 | 33% | 1,848 |
| 5+ | 14% | 784 |

### What this tells us:

- **88% of respondents have faced at least one legal issue in the past 2 years.** This aligns with ABA data showing 50% of households face a legal issue annually.
- **33% faced 3-5 issues** — these are CaseMate's power users. At $20/month, they'd use the service multiple times, deepening their profile and increasing switching costs.
- **14% faced 5+ issues** — these users would benefit most from CaseMate's persistent memory. Each new issue builds on the profile from previous ones.
- The average respondent faces **2.8 legal issues per 2-year period**, or roughly **1.4 per year**.

### Revenue implication:

Users who face multiple legal issues are the highest-retention subscribers. They experience the compounding value of memory (CaseMate gets smarter about their situation over time) and are least likely to churn.

## Qualitative Research: DM & Comment Analysis

We analyzed **400+ direct messages and comments** across TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook from users who engaged with our legal content. These are unsolicited messages from real people describing real legal problems.

### Top Pain Points by Category

| Category | % of Messages | Sample Quote |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Landlord / security deposit** | 28% | "My landlord is keeping $1,400 and won't tell me what it's for. What can I do?" |
| **Employment / wages** | 19% | "I think my employer is misclassifying me as a contractor to avoid paying benefits" |
| **Debt collection** | 15% | "Collectors keep calling me at work even though I told them to stop. Is that legal?" |
| **Lease issues** | 12% | "My landlord wants to raise rent $400 mid-lease. Can they do that?" |
| **Small claims** | 10% | "Someone owes me $3,000 and won't pay. How do I take them to small claims court?" |
| **Traffic / tickets** | 8% | "I got a ticket but the speed limit sign was hidden. Can I fight it?" |
| **Family law** | 5% | "Going through a custody battle and can't afford an attorney" |
| **Other** | 3% | Various consumer rights, contract disputes, immigration questions |

### Common Themes

1. **"I don't know where to start."** — The most common sentiment. People don't know which laws apply, what their rights are, or what steps to take. CaseMate's guided workflows and Know Your Rights library directly address this.

2. **"I can't afford a lawyer for this."** — Nearly every message includes a cost barrier. People know they need help but feel the issue isn't "worth" $349/hour. CaseMate makes every issue worth addressing at $20/month.

3. **"I wish I had known this sooner."** — Many messages describe situations where knowing their rights earlier would have prevented or reduced harm. CaseMate's proactive deadline detection and rights education address this.

4. **"Can you help me write a letter?"** — Demand letters are the #1 specific action people request. They know a formal letter carries weight but don't know how to write one with proper legal citations. CaseMate's letter generator is built for exactly this.

5. **"Does this apply in my state?"** — People are aware that laws vary by state but don't know how to find state-specific information. CaseMate's 50-state legal knowledge base solves this at the infrastructure level.

CaseMate is not solving a hypothetical problem. **Over 10,000 real people** participated in our research across polls, surveys, DMs, and comments. The data consistently shows:

1. **The problem is massive** — 78% of our audience has needed a lawyer they couldn't afford

This research directly shaped every product decision, pricing tier, and marketing message. CaseMate was built for these people, validated by these people, and will be launched to these people.
